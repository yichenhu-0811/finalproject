An interactive app that enriches visitor experiences and supports local businesses by showing community history, updating and preserving archives, connecting users with the area's heritage and economy, and ensuring the legacy’s continuation.
When travelers visit a new city, they typically focus on visiting popular landmarks and attending well-known events. While these experiences may have led to great times, they have missed the opportunity to truly have an immersive experience with the city and its people.

There is often limited interaction between a visitor and a local, resulting in the visitor having a shallow understanding of the city's history and culture. Without fully experiencing the city, the visitor did not gain a deeper appreciation for how the city evolved and become what it is today.

Downtown Ann Arbor Historical Street Exhibit

The information boards around downtown provide an engaging and informative experience for visitors and locals by showcasing the city's historical events, people, and landmarks. Through sharing these stories, it fosters a greater appreciation and understanding of Ann Arbors' cultural heritage and city history.
Black Bottom & Lafayette Park with Marsha Music

The QR codes around Lafayette Park allowed an immersive and enriching experience. It was a way for the city and Marsha Music to raise awareness of the impacts of urban renewal and use it as a way to preserve history and celebrate the neighborhoods' legacy.
